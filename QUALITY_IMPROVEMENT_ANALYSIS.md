### Introduction

- Take-home (taxes): $3,400/month NET ‚Üê This is what you budget!

### Section 2: Fixed vs Variable Expenses
**Maya's Fixed Expenses (same every month):**
- Rent: $1,200
- Car payment: $300
- Insurance: $400
- Student loan: $250
- Internet/phone: $100
Total fixed: $2,250 (66% of income)

**Maya's Variable Expenses (change monthly):**
- Groceries: $300-400
- Gas: $100-150
- Entertainment: $100-200
- Clothing: $50-150
Total variable: ~$700

**Maya's Savings Buffer:** $3,400 - $2,250 - $700 = $450/month to savings/goals

### Section 3: The 50/30/20 Rule (for women earning $3,000-$5,000)
- 50% Needs (housing, food, insurance, utilities, debt)
- 30% Wants (entertainment, dining out, hobbies)
- 20% Savings & Goals (emergency fund, retirement, dreams)

**Example:**
- On $3,400 net: Needs = $1,700, Wants = $1,020, Savings = $680

### Section 4: Common Women's Budgeting Challenges
**1. Caregiving expenses (not in "standard" budgets)**
   - Childcare: can be $800-2,000/month
   - Elder parent support: variable
   - Solution: Create "Care" category (10-15% of budget)

**2. Irregular income (freelancers, part-time)**
   - Month A: $4,500, Month B: $3,200
   - Solution: Average last 12 months, budget low months

**3. Budget guilt (spending on self feels wasteful)**
   - Research: Women spend more on family, less on self
   - Solution: 30% "Wants" category is EARNED, not guilty

### Section 5: Creating Your Budget in 5 Steps

**Step 1:** Track ONE week of spending
```
Day 1 Latte: $5, Lunch: $12, Gas: $20
Day 2 Groceries: $45, Netflix: $15
...
```

**Step 2:** Categorize into Fixed/Variable
**Step 3:** Calculate your "Needs" number
**Step 4:** Allocate remaining to Wants + Savings
**Step 5:** Build accountability (monthly check-in)

### Key Takeaways
- Your budget = your financial roadmap, not a punishment
- Know your NET income first (after taxes)
- Fixed + Variable + Savings should equal net income
- Review and adjust monthly (life changes!)
- Women: Don't shortchange the "Wants" category

## WOMEN-CENTRIC CONTENT PRINCIPLES

### Principle 1: Context Before Theory
**‚ùå Wrong:** "Compound interest is when interest earned also earns interest"
**‚úÖ Right:** "If you invest $5,000 at age 25 earning 7%/year, by age 65 it's worth $148,000. Your $5,000 turned into $143,000 in GROWTH just by waiting. That's the power of compound interest."

### Principle 2: Real Life Scenarios
Include case studies with:
- Character name (relatable)
- Age and life stage
- Income level
- Specific challenge
- Step-by-step solution
- Real numbers

### Principle 3: Address Emotional Barriers
Women often have:
- Money shame/guilt
- Fear of investing
- Perfectionistic budgeting
- "Not smart enough" feelings
- Negotiation anxiety

**Address these directly:** "It's normal to feel anxious about investing. You're not alone."

### Principle 4: Link to Women's Values
Research: Women prioritize financial security for family, education, and independence.
**Frame lessons around:** family protection, education funding, independence goals

### Principle 5: Actionable Steps
Every lesson should answer: "What's my next step TODAY?"

Not: "You should learn about 401ks"
But: "Call your HR department TODAY and ask for 401k plan documents. Text them to yourself so you don't forget."
