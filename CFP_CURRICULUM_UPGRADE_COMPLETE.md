## Content Structure (Applied to All Lessons)

Each lesson now includes:

### 1. Learning Objectives (5-7 items)
Measurable outcomes aligned to CFP standards. Example:
- "Understand the relationship between risk, time horizon, and expected returns"
- "Calculate basic investment returns and evaluate performance vs. benchmarks"
- "Build a simple investment plan aligned to life stages"

### 2. Introduction
Professional-level hook explaining why this topic matters. Evidence-based framing (e.g., "Over 30 years, $10k in savings earning 0.5% grows to $11.6k; the same amount in a diversified portfolio earning 7% grows to $76k—a difference of $64.5k").

### 3. Definitions (5-10 items)
Glossary of essential terminology with clear, non-jargon definitions. Example:
- **Time Horizon**: "The number of years until you need the money. Longer horizons (10+ years) can tolerate more stock volatility."

### 4. Sections (4-7 content sections)
Deep instructional content covering:
- Core principles with evidence-based strategies
- Real-world scenarios with calculations
- Common mistakes and how to avoid them
- Action steps and implementation guidance

### 5. Key Takeaways (5 items)
Bullet-point summary of most critical lessons.

### 6. Action Items (5 items)
Specific next steps for implementation.

### 7. Checklist (10 items)
Downloadable-style checklist for self-assessment and tracking.

### 8. Practice Exercise
Step-by-step exercise to apply lesson to personal finances.

### 9. Resources (4+ items)
Links to SEC, CFPB, Bogleheads, professional associations, and tools.

### 10. Quiz (5 questions)
Assessment questions to verify understanding.
