### **Module 8: Financial Security & Legacy Planning** (4 hours)

_Protecting what you've built_

#### Lessons:

1. **Estate Planning Essentials** (60 min)
   - Objectives:
     - Understand why estate planning is essential for everyone, not just the wealthy.
     - Learn the basic components: wills, trusts, powers of attorney, and healthcare directives.
     - Identify action steps to create or update your estate plan.
   - Topics covered:
     - Why everyone needs estate planning: protecting loved ones, avoiding probate, controlling asset distribution, reducing family conflict.
     - Wills and trusts basics: difference between wills and living trusts, when each is appropriate, revocable vs. irrevocable trusts.
     - Beneficiary designations: how they override wills, reviewing and updating beneficiaries on retirement accounts, life insurance, and bank accounts.
     - Power of attorney (financial and healthcare): durable POA vs. limited POA, healthcare proxy, selecting trusted agents.
     - Living wills and healthcare directives: end-of-life wishes, DNR orders, organ donation preferences.
     - Digital assets and passwords: creating a digital estate plan, password managers, social media legacy contacts, cryptocurrency and online accounts.
   - Activities & exercises:
     - Checklist: Complete an estate planning readiness assessment (15 questions).
     - Exercise: Draft a list of all accounts with beneficiary designations and verify they're current.
     - Worksheet: Identify 2-3 trusted individuals for POA and healthcare proxy roles.
   - Assessment:
     - Quiz: 10 questions on wills, trusts, beneficiaries, and POA basics.
   - Resources:
     - State-specific estate planning requirements (links by state)
     - Sample will and trust comparison chart
     - Digital estate planning template and password vault recommendations

2. **Life Insurance Deep Dive** (60 min)
   - Objectives:
     - Learn how to calculate appropriate life insurance coverage for your situation.
     - Understand the differences between term and permanent life insurance.
     - Know when to review and update your coverage.
   - Topics covered:
     - How much coverage do you need?: income replacement method, DIME method (Debt, Income, Mortgage, Education), special considerations for stay-at-home parents and caregivers.
     - Term vs. whole life insurance: term (affordable, temporary coverage), whole/universal/variable life (permanent, cash value component), pros and cons of each.
     - Understanding policy details: premiums, death benefit, riders (disability waiver, accelerated death benefit, child term rider), exclusions and contestability periods.
     - Naming beneficiaries: primary vs. contingent, per stirpes vs. per capita, avoiding common mistakes (minor children, estate as beneficiary).
     - Insurance through work vs. individual: portability, coverage limits, supplemental policies, conversion options when leaving a job.
     - When to update your coverage: marriage, divorce, birth/adoption, home purchase, significant income change, retirement.
   - Activities & exercises:
     - Calculator: Use the DIME method to estimate your personal life insurance needs.
     - Comparison tool: Evaluate term vs. whole life for a sample scenario (interactive).
     - Action item: Review your current life insurance policies and beneficiary designations.
   - Assessment:
     - Case study: Recommend appropriate life insurance for 3 different life situations (single, young family, pre-retiree).
   - Resources:
     - Life insurance needs calculator
     - Policy comparison checklist
     - Sample beneficiary designation form and instructions

3. **Protecting Your Identity & Assets** (45 min)
   - Objectives:
     - Understand common identity-theft vectors and how to reduce risk.
     - Learn practical cybersecurity habits to protect online financial accounts.
     - Know how to detect fraud early and the steps to recover.
     - Identify protections for vulnerable adults and estate-related safeguards.
   - Topics covered:
     - Identity theft prevention: secure mail, credit freezes, monitoring services, minimizing data sharing.
     - Cybersecurity for finances: strong passwords, password managers, multi-factor authentication (MFA), secure Wi‚ÄëFi, device hygiene.
     - Fraud detection and response: red flags, how to contact banks/credit bureaus, filing FTC reports, police reports, documenting losses.
     - Protecting vulnerable adults: guardianship basics, limited powers of attorney, trusted contact persons, joint-account risks.
     - Insurance and legal tools for asset protection: umbrella policies, liability coverage, and when to consult an attorney.
     - Emergency document storage: secure physical and digital vaults, what to store (titles, deeds, account lists, passwords) and how to share safely with trustees/executors.
   - Activities & exercises:
     - Walkthrough: enable MFA on a primary financial account (demo checklist).
     - Exercise: Create a personalized emergency document inventory and storage plan.
     - Quiz: Identify phishing and scam examples (5 questions).
   - Resources:
     - FTC IdentityTheft.gov checklist
     - Sample letter to fraud departments and credit bureaus
     - Recommended password managers and secure storage patterns

4. **Building Generational Wealth** (45 min)
   - Objectives:
     - Explore practical steps to pass on financial resources and values.
     - Understand vehicles for legacy planning (trusts, tax-efficient giving, education funds).
     - Learn how to have family conversations that transmit values and financial literacy.
   - Topics covered:
     - Teaching children and young adults: age-appropriate allowances, investing basics, and stewardship.
     - Giving and philanthropy: donor-advised funds, charitable remainder trusts, tax implications, and strategic giving.
     - Trusts and transfer vehicles: revocable vs. irrevocable trusts, beneficiary designations, 529 college savings plans, and when each makes sense.
     - Tax-efficient wealth transfer basics: estate tax thresholds, gifting strategies, and lifetime exclusion concepts (high level, non-advisory).
     - Family governance and values: family mission statements, legacy letters, intergenerational meetings, and succession planning for family businesses.
   - Activities & exercises:
     - Template: Draft a one-page legacy letter to heirs that includes values, practical wishes, and financial guidance.
     - Case study: Compare outcomes of a simple will vs. a trust for a mid-size estate.
   - Assessment:
     - Short reflection exercise (submitable): What 3 financial values will you pass to your heirs and how?
   - Resources:
     - Guide to basic trust types (non-legal educational overview)
     - Sample family meeting agenda and conversation prompts
   - Values-based financial planning

5. **Financial Confidence & Advocacy** (30 min)
   - Objectives:
     - Build the mindset and skills to confidently manage, ask about, and advocate for financial needs.
     - Identify sources of reliable financial information and professional support.
   - Topics covered:
     - Taking charge of your finances: establishing routines, delegating safely, and tracking progress.
     - Effective communication with financial professionals: how to prepare for meetings, what questions to ask, and how to compare advisors.
     - Asking questions without shame: role-play scripts for common situations (pay negotiation, checking statements, calling insurers).
     - Building a support network: trusted peers, mentors, community resources, and finding CFP¬Æ professionals when needed.
     - Advocating for financial equity: workplace compensation conversations, rights when facing discrimination, and community-level engagement.
     - Next steps & continuing development: setting a 90-day action plan and identifying 1‚Äì2 measurable goals.
   - Activities & exercises:
     - Role-play: Prepare a 5-minute script to negotiate a raise or ask for better benefits (template provided).
     - Action plan: Create a 90-day financial confidence plan with weekly checkpoints.
     - Final self-assessment: Re-take the Financial Wellness Assessment to measure progress.
   - Resources:
     - Question checklist for financial advisor interviews
     - Negotiation scripts and salary-research tools (links)
     - Community groups and mentor-matching suggestions

**Module Quiz:** 30 min  
**Final Course Assessment:** 30 min
