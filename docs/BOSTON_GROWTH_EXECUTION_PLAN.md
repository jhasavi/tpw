# Boston Women's Empowerment Growth Plan

## Objective

Make The Purple Wings the most trusted women's financial empowerment nonprofit in Greater Boston by building a clear growth engine around four audiences:

- Women who need practical financial education
- Volunteers who can teach, mentor, and organize
- Donors who want measurable local impact
- Partners who can expand reach and credibility

## Positioning

The Purple Wings should be presented as more than a course library. It should be positioned as a Boston-area movement that helps women gain financial confidence, build community, and access free education designed for their real lives.

## 90-Day Plan

### Phase 1: Clarify The Public Story

- Lead with a simple promise: free financial education for women, rooted in Greater Boston.
- Put donor, volunteer, and partner entry points next to learner entry points.
- Turn media proof, founder story, and event history into trust builders on high-traffic pages.

### Phase 2: Build The Conversion Engine

- Launch a dedicated get-involved funnel page.
- Create one clear CTA for each audience: learn, volunteer, donate, partner.
- Route donor and volunteer inquiries into prefilled contact flows.
- Add recurring mentions of Boston, Needham, and Massachusetts community impact.

### Phase 3: Build Local Momentum

- Run monthly community workshops in Greater Boston.
- Recruit volunteer educators, content reviewers, and event hosts.
- Package sponsorship opportunities for banks, employers, and women's organizations.
- Collect and publish real stories from participants and supporters.

## Execution Priorities

### 1. Audience Growth

- Publish weekly content tied to real financial decisions women face.
- Promote life-stage learning journeys for women in their 20s, 30s, 40s, 50s, and beyond.
- Capture email subscribers from every major landing page.

### 2. Volunteer Growth

- Offer clear volunteer roles: workshop educator, mentor, event host, content reviewer, community ambassador.
- Use a lightweight intake flow through the contact page until a dedicated volunteer form exists.
- Highlight volunteers publicly to create social proof.

### 3. Donor Growth

- Connect every donation ask to a concrete outcome.
- Show what donations fund: free workshops, translated materials, learner scholarships, Boston outreach.
- Add a real payment link as soon as available and support recurring giving.

### 4. Partnership Growth

- Target Boston-area employers, banks, real estate firms, universities, and nonprofits.
- Sell education-first partnerships with measurable impact instead of generic sponsorship.
- Use media mentions and local event history as proof of credibility.

## Core Metrics

- New women reached each month
- Email subscribers added each month
- Volunteer inquiries per month
- Donation inquiries and completed gifts per month
- Number of active Boston-area partners
- Event registrations and attendance
- Stories collected from women served
